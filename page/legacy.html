MP. Harsh Vardhan Ji

Harsh Vardhan Ji, a prominent Indian politician from Uttar Pradesh, had held several significant positions in his political career. He began his political career with the Socialist Party but later switched to the Congress party.

He was elected as a Member of Parliament (MP) twice from the Maharajganj constituency in Uttar Pradesh, first in 1989 and then again in 2009. He had also been a member of the Uttar Pradesh Legislative Assembly, where he served as a Minister of State for several portfolios such as health, agriculture, and rural development.

Harsh Vardhan Ji was born on December 1 1947, in the city of Gorakhpur, located in the state of Uttar Pradesh, India. He was born into a family with a political background, his father, Sri Rampyare Lal Ji, was also a prominent political leader who served as a member of the Uttar Pradesh Legislative Assembly. Harsh Vardhan Ji received his early education at the Lucknow Sainik School, a prestigious military school in Uttar Pradesh, India.

After completing his schooling, he went on to earn a Bachelor of Arts degree from Lucknow University, also located in Uttar Pradesh. Following his academic pursuits, Harsh Vardhan Ji began his career in politics and soon became a prominent figure in Indian politics.

He went on to hold several key positions within his party and contributed significantly to Indian politics throughout his career. Supriya Shrinate, Harsh Vardhan's daughter, was an executive editor at ET Now, a business channel of Times Television Network, before resigning to contest the 2019 Lok Sabha elections from Maharajganj. She was later appointed as the spokesperson for the Indian National Congress (INC) and presently the chairman of social media and communication department.

Apart from his political career, Harsh Vardhan Ji was known for his philanthropic work and his contributions to social causes. He was associated with several charitable organizations and worked towards improving the lives of underprivileged people in India.

Unfortunately, Harsh Vardhan Ji passed away on October 4, 2016, due to a heart attack. His contributions to Indian politics and society continue to be remembered and celebrated by his supporters and colleagues.
